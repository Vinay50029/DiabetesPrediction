Introduction

Diabetes is one of the most widespread chronic diseases, affecting millions of people globally. If not detected early, it can lead to serious complications. Early diagnosis and regular monitoring are key to preventing long-term damage. This project introduces an intelligent prediction system that uses Artificial Intelligence (AI) to assist in the early detection of diabetes and diabetic retinopathy. The system works in two ways:

  • By analyzing user-reported symptoms to detect the possibility of diabetes
  • By examining retina images to identify and classify the stage of diabetic retinopathy

How Prediction Works ?

Symptom-Based Diabetes Prediction

Users fill out a simple form by entering details such as common symptoms related to diabetes. The system uses the inputs to assess the likelihood of diabetes based on patterns learned from existing medical data. The prediction is generated using a trained AI model that studies relationships among various symptoms. It provides a result indicating whether the person is likely to be diabetic or not. As a result, it can predict diabetes with an accuracy of 96.1% *These results may not be 100% sure but the model predicts good results upto 96%.

.

Retina Image-Based Diabetic Retinopathy Detection

In this method, users upload retina images of both eyes. The system carefully examines these images to detect the presence and severity of diabetic retinopathy. Based on visual patterns and features found in the eye, the system can classify the condition into one of the following stages:

  • No Diabetic Retinopathy
  • Mild Stage
  • Moderate Stage
  • Severe Stage
  • Proliferative Stage

This visual screening tool is built using deep learning techniques and has been trained on a large dataset of retina images. It provides fast and reliable stage-wise predictions, helping users become aware of their potential eye health status. It is capable of predicting the correct stage with an accuracy of 96.3% *These results may not be 100% sure but the model predicts good results upto 96%.
